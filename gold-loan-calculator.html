What is a Gold Loan?

A gold loan is a secured loan where you pledge your gold jewelry or ornaments as collateral to get instant funds from banks or financial institutions. The loan amount depends on the value of your gold and the LTV ratio set by the lender.

Gold loans are popular because they offer quick approval, minimal documentation, and lower interest rates compared to personal loans.

Understanding Loan-to-Value (LTV)

Loan-to-Value (LTV) is the percentage of the gold value that a lender is willing to provide as a loan. According to RBI guidelines, the maximum LTV for gold loans is usually up to 75%.

For example, if your gold value is ₹100,000 and the LTV is 75%, you can get a loan of ₹75,000.
