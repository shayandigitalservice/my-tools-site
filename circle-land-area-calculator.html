What is a Circular Land Area?

A circular land area refers to a piece of land that has a round shape, similar to a circle. This type of land is commonly found in gardens, parks, irrigation fields, circular farms, and water reservoirs. Because of the round shape, traditional rectangular land formulas cannot be used to calculate the total area.

To measure circular land accurately, a mathematical formula based on the radius of the circle is used. This formula allows you to determine the total area of the land quickly and precisely.

Circle Area Formula

The formula used to calculate the area of a circle is:

Area = π × r²

Where:

  • π (Pi) is approximately equal to 3.1416
  • r represents the radius of the circle

The radius is the distance from the center of the circle to its edge. Once the radius is known, the total land area can be calculated using the formula above.

Land Area Units Explained

The circle land calculator converts the calculated area into multiple commonly used land measurement units. These units include:

Square Feet (Sq Ft)

Square feet is one of the most widely used units in real estate and residential property measurement. It is commonly used for plots, houses, and small land areas.

Square Meter (Sq M)

Square meters are part of the international metric system. Many countries use square meters for property documentation and land measurement.

Katha

Katha is a traditional land measurement unit used in several South Asian countries including India, Nepal, and Bangladesh. It is commonly used in rural areas.

Bigha

Bigha is another traditional unit used to measure agricultural land. Farmers often use bigha to describe the size of farmland or fields.

Acre

An acre is an internationally recognized land measurement unit commonly used for measuring large plots and agricultural land.
