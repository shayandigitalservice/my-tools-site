4. Deep-Dive into Meghalaya Land Metrics and Local Administration

Land administration in Meghalaya is uniquely structured, governed by customary laws alongside tribal and Autonomous District Councils—namely the Khasi Hills Autonomous District Council (KHADC), Garo Hills Autonomous District Council (GHADC), and Jaintia Hills Autonomous District Council (JHADC). These councils oversee boundary line recognition, allotment guidelines, traditional land tenures (such as Ri-Kynti and Ri-Raid), and non-tribal transfer restrictions under the Meghalaya Transfer of Land (Regulation) Act.

Because transactions are closely tracked by regional bodies and surveyor frameworks, minor math discrepancies in deed formatting can cause verification issues or delayed approvals during property evaluation. Standardizing units across different regions remains vital for clarity. For systematic mapping, 1 Hectare is defined as exactly 10,000 square meters, 1 Acre contains 43,560 square feet, and 1 Bigha translates to 17,424 square feet (20 Kathas). Our tool follows this precise equation to make sure your paperwork matches structured analytical systems perfectly.
