What is Simple Interest?

Simple interest is the interest calculated only on the original principal amount. Unlike compound interest, it does not include interest on previously earned interest. This makes it easy to calculate and understand.

Simple Interest Formula

Simple Interest = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100

  • Principal (P) = Initial amount of money
  • Rate (R) = Interest rate per year
  • Time (T) = Time period in years

Simple Interest vs Compound Interest

Simple interest and compound interest are two different methods of calculating interest.

  • Simple Interest: Calculated only on principal
  • Compound Interest: Calculated on principal + accumulated interest

For long-term investments, compound interest is more beneficial, but for short-term calculations, simple interest is commonly used.

Examples of Simple Interest Calculation

Example 1

If you invest ₹10,000 at an interest rate of 5% per year for 2 years:

Interest = (10000 × 5 × 2) / 100 = ₹1000 Total Amount = ₹11,000

Example 2
