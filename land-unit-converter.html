Common Land Measurement Units

There are many different land measurement units used around the world. Below are some of the most common units used for measuring land area.

Square Feet (Sq Ft)

Square feet is one of the most widely used land measurement units in real estate. It is commonly used for residential plots, houses, and small land areas. One acre is equal to 43,560 square feet.

Square Meter (Sq M)

Square meters are part of the metric system and are widely used internationally. Many countries use square meters for land registration and official property records.

Katha

Katha is a traditional land measurement unit used in India, Nepal, and Bangladesh. The value of one katha may vary depending on the region, but it is commonly used in rural areas for measuring agricultural land.

Bigha

Bigha is another traditional land unit used widely in South Asia. Farmers often use bigha to describe farmland size. However, the exact size of one bigha can vary depending on the state or region.

Acre

An acre is a widely recognized international land measurement unit used for measuring large plots and farmland. It is commonly used in agriculture and property documentation.

Hectare

A hectare is part of the metric system and is commonly used for measuring large areas of land. One hectare equals 10,000 square meters or approximately 2.47 acres.
