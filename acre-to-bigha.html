What is an Acre?

An acre is one of the most widely used units for measuring land area. It is commonly used in agriculture, real estate, and property documentation in many countries. One acre is equal to 43,560 square feet or approximately 4046.86 square meters.

The acre is part of the imperial measurement system and has been used for centuries to measure farmland, fields, and large plots of land. Because it is recognized internationally, many land records and government documents use acres to describe land size.

What is Bigha?

Bigha is a traditional unit of land measurement used mainly in South Asia. It is commonly used in rural areas where agriculture is the primary occupation. Farmers often use bigha to describe the size of their farmland or agricultural plots.

The value of one bigha can vary slightly depending on the region. Different states or districts may have different measurements for a single bigha. Despite these variations, it remains a popular and widely recognized unit in rural communities.
