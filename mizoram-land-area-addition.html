Let's understand how our automated software engine handles standard revenue calculations on the backend. Suppose you have two distinct land plots under a single ownership pass that need to be aggregated for a mutation deed:

  • Property Fragment 1: 1 Acre and 65 Decimal
  • Property Fragment 2: 2 Acre and 55 Decimal

Step-by-step Execution Process:

  1. Sum the Decimal Units: 65 Decimal + 55 Decimal = 120 Decimal.
  2. Apply Carry Logic: Since 120 Decimal exceeds the standard 100 limit per Acre, the algorithm calculates: 120 - 100 = 20 Decimal remaining. 1 full Acre is safely carried over to the primary Acre stack.
  3. Sum the Acre Units: 1 Acre + 2 Acre + 1 Acre (Carried Over from Decimals) = 4 Acres.
  4. Final Consolidated Result: The engine pushes out exactly 4 Acre and 20 Decimal. It then automatically maps this value into auxiliary values like 10.5 Bighas, 73,180.8 square feet, or 6,798.24 square meters.

4. Deep-Dive into Mizoram Revenue Land Metrics and Law

Land administration in Mizoram is legally shaped by historic frameworks like the Mizoram Land Revenue Act. These laws govern the transfer, boundary demarcation, and processing of land across the state. Because these transactions are heavily scrutinized by Land Revenue Officers, Revenue Inspectors (RI), and local councils, even minor math discrepancies in deed formatting can cause a registration or mutation to be rejected.

Furthermore, standardizing units across different regions remains an issue. For instance, in parts of rural and urban settlements, the local definition of Bigha varies. However, for formal state valuation and digital records, state systems match 1 Acre to exactly 100 Decimals and sets the regional Bigha value to exactly 40 Decimals (meaning 1 Acre contains 2.5 Bighas). Our tool follows this strict state revenue equation to make sure your paperwork matches official data structures perfectly.
