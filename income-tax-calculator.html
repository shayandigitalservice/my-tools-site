What is Income Tax in India?

Income tax is a direct tax imposed by the government on the income earned by individuals and businesses. The amount of tax payable depends on your income slab and the tax regime you choose. In India, taxpayers can choose between two tax systems: the old tax regime and the new tax regime. Each system has different tax rates and deduction rules.

The old tax regime allows several deductions and exemptions such as Section 80C investments, house rent allowance (HRA), medical insurance deductions, and other tax-saving benefits. The new tax regime, introduced by the government, offers lower tax rates but removes most deductions and exemptions.

Old Tax Regime vs New Tax Regime

Choosing between the old tax regime and the new tax regime is an important decision for taxpayers. The old tax regime allows multiple deductions and exemptions which can significantly reduce your taxable income if you invest in tax-saving instruments.

On the other hand, the new tax regime offers lower tax rates but removes most deductions. This system is beneficial for taxpayers who do not claim many deductions or prefer a simple tax structure without investment requirements.

Using the income tax calculator helps you compare both systems easily so you can make an informed decision based on your financial situation.

Common Tax Saving Options

There are several ways to legally reduce your income tax liability in India. Some popular tax-saving options include investments under Section 80C such as PPF, ELSS, life insurance premiums, employee provident fund contributions, and tax-saving fixed deposits.

You can also claim deductions for health insurance premiums under Section 80D, home loan interest under Section 24, and HRA exemption if you live in a rented house. Planning these deductions early in the financial year can significantly reduce your tax burden.
