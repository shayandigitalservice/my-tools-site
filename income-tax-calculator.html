What is Income Tax in India?

Income tax is a direct tax levied by the Government of India on the income earned by individuals and businesses. The amount of tax payable depends on your income slab and the tax regime you choose. The government has introduced two types of tax regimes to give taxpayers flexibility in managing their finances.

The old tax regime allows various deductions and exemptions such as Section 80C, HRA, medical insurance, and more. The new tax regime offers lower tax rates but removes most deductions, making it simpler but sometimes less beneficial depending on your investments.

Old Tax Regime vs New Tax Regime

Old Tax Regime

The old tax regime allows taxpayers to reduce their taxable income through various deductions and exemptions. Some common deductions include:

  • Section 80C (PPF, LIC, ELSS, EPF)
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA)
  • Standard Deduction
  • Medical Insurance (80D)

This regime is beneficial for individuals who actively invest in tax-saving instruments.

New Tax Regime

The new tax regime offers lower tax rates but removes most deductions. It is ideal for individuals who do not want to invest in tax-saving schemes and prefer a simpler calculation method.

Choosing the right regime depends on your financial situation, and this calculator helps you compare both options easily.

Tax Saving Tips for 2026

If you are using the old tax regime, you can reduce your tax liability by investing in tax-saving instruments:

  • Public Provident Fund (PPF)
  • Equity Linked Saving Scheme (ELSS)
  • Life Insurance (LIC)
  • Tax Saving Fixed Deposits

You can also claim deductions for health insurance, home loan interest, and education loans to further reduce your taxable income.
