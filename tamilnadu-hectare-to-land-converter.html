Tamil Nadu (TN) state land registration operations follow a structured digital infrastructure managed under the Commissionerate of Land Administration. Whenever property owners access the official digital portal Anyror Patta Chitta to retrieve critical legal ownership data like Patta (Land Ownership Certificate) or Chitta (Land Revenue Ledger Accounts), they will observe that the area metric is uniformly displayed in Hectare (Ha) and Are. 2. Decoding TN Land Metrics Hierarchy: Cent, Ground, and Hectare Scales

Analyzing the land revenue guidelines of Tamil Nadu reveals that regional measurements vary between urban developments and agricultural land assets. Understanding these conversions enables property buyers to verify any Patta sketch or Fard layout directly without relying heavily on state surveyors or Village Administrative Officers (VAO).

According to established Tamil Nadu land tracking standards: Agricultural holdings are measured using **Cents**. One standard Cent represents exactly **435.6 Square Feet** of spatial area. Therefore, 1 full Acre comprises precisely 100 Cents, yielding an area of **43,560 Square Feet** ($100 \times 435.6$). Conversely, commercial real estate layout specifications and housing plots throughout Chennai and major municipalities utilize the **Ground** metric. One traditional Ground defines a spatial blueprint of **2,400 Square Feet**. When scaled against larger tracking standards, approximately 18.15 Grounds compose 1 clean Acre. 3. Understanding Legal Pillars: Patta, Chitta, Adangal, and FMB Sketches

Converting metrics provides numerical clarity, but verifying property title deeds requires understanding the essential components of Tamil Nadu land documentation:

A. Patta (பட்டா): This is the ultimate legal land ownership document issued by the Tahsildar in the name of the absolute property handler. It includes vital cross-references such as District, Taluk, Village name, Patta Number, and Survey Number descriptions.

B. Chitta (சிட்டா): A detailed revenue ledger certificate showing the classification of the land holding—whether it falls under Nanjai (wetland/irrigated land) or Punjai (dryland/rain-fed land).

C. Adangal (அடங்கல்):Maintained directly by the Village Administrative Officer (VAO), this register tracks real-time crop cultivation details, property value conditions, and current land use on the parcel.

D. FMB Sketch (Field Measurement Book): A critical compilation containing precise boundary sketch dimensions mapped directly by the survey department to resolve physical land encroachments.
