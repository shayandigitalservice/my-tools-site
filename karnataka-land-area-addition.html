3. Real-World Practical Example (Custom Carry Arithmetic)

Let's understand how our automated software engine handles standard revenue calculations on the backend. Suppose you have two distinct Hissa plots under a single Survey Number that need to be aggregated for a mutation (Podium) deed:

  • Property Fragment 1: 1 Acre, 35 Gunta, and 3 Anna
  • Property Fragment 2: 2 Acre, 15 Gunta, and 2 Anna

Step-by-step Execution Process:

  1. Sum the Anna Units: 3 Anna + 2 Anna = 5 Anna. Since 4 Anna equals 1 Gunta, the algorithm sets 5 - 4 = 1 Anna as remaining, and 1 Gunta carries forward.
  2. Sum the Gunta Units: 35 Gunta + 15 Gunta + 1 Gunta (Carried Over) = 51 Gunta. Since 40 Gunta equals 1 Acre, the algorithm processes: 51 - 40 = 11 Gunta remaining. 1 full Acre is safely carried over to the primary Acre stack.
  3. Sum the Acre Units: 1 Acre + 2 Acre + 1 Acre (Carried Over) = 4 Acres.
  4. Final Consolidated Result: The engine pushes out exactly 4 Acre, 11 Gunta, and 1 Anna. It then automatically maps this value into auxiliary values like 1,86,119 Square Feet or 17,291 Square Meters.

4. Deep-Dive into Karnataka Revenue Land Metrics and Law

Land administration in Karnataka is legally shaped by historic frameworks like the Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964. These laws govern the transfer, conversion, and processing of agricultural lands. Because these transactions are heavily scrutinized by Tahsildars, Revenue Inspectors, and Village Accountants, minor math discrepancies in deed formatting can cause a mutation registration to be systematically rejected.

Furthermore, standardizing units across different regions remains an issue. For instance, in coastal districts like Mangalore or Udupi, local terms vary slightly from North Karnataka regions. However, for formal state valuation, Bhoomi matches 1 Acre to exactly 40 Guntas, and sets 1 Gunta to 1,089 Square Feet.
