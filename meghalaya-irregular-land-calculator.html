1. Professional Overview of Irregular Land Forms in Meghalaya

Meghalaya's structural geographic architecture features steep hill terrains, complex private community plots, and riverine forest zones across East Khasi Hills, West Garo Hills, and Jaintia Hills that prevent conventional uniform symmetrical layouts. Straight rectangular layouts are rare. Instead, field blocks exist primarily as highly distorted, irregular non-parallel quadrilaterals.

Traditional computational engineering shortcuts, such as the inaccurate Average Method (adding opposing parameters and taking means), fail dramatically in hill context maps. These mathematical shortfalls bypass angular elevations and lateral distortion variables, producing highly exaggerated values that violate the parameters of the official Directorate of Land Records Meghalaya matrix.

The triangulation blueprint structure decomposes the irregular quadrilateral configuration into two separate component geometry blocks. By recording the structural dynamic pivot line E, we can calculate each spatial segment using the classical Heron equations system layout:

s = (Side X + Side Y + Side Z) / 2
